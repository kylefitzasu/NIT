🏌 Format
  • Modified Scotch format — partners alternate shots after the tee shot
  • Dynamic GHIN-based handicaps updated daily — always current
  • Match play scoring — holes won/lost, not total strokes
⛳ Tee Boxes
Default course is Lake/Valley rotation at The Pinery Country Club, Blue tees (Rating 70.8 · Slope 131). Teams may mutually agree to play a different rotation.
🎲 How Handicap Strokes Work
Step 1 — Each player's Course Handicap is calculated from the Blue tees using:
Course Handicap = HI × (131 ÷ 113) + (70.8 − 72)
Step 2 — Add both players' Course Handicaps together, then divide by 2. Any half stroke rounds up to the nearest whole number.
Team Handicap = Round↑((CH Player 1 + CH Player 2) ÷ 2)
Step 3 — The higher-handicap team receives the difference in strokes. Strokes are applied on the hardest holes by handicap rating (hole 1 = hardest, hole 18 = easiest).

Example: Team A has CH 4 & 8 → Team Handicap = 6. Team B has CH 2 & 3 → Team Handicap = 3. Team A receives 3 strokes (6 − 3).

📅 Playoff Qualification
  • Top 8 teams by total points across all divisions qualify
  • Seeded 1–8 by total points accumulated during the regular season
  • Ties broken by head-to-head record, then wins, then extra hole wins
  • 7 remaining teams do not advance to playoffs

⚠ 2026 Important Rules — Please Read Before Committing

All players must agree to the following before committing to the 2026 season.

#1
Scheduling Disputes

Any scheduling disputes will be settled between the teams. Please don’t reach out to the commissioner with your scheduling woes.

#2
Match Deadlines — No Exceptions

Only 1 match per month. Matches must be played within the assigned month. If both teams agree, they may reschedule to a later month — but ALL matches must be completed by August 31st. Any match not played results in 0 points for both teams. No exceptions.

#3
Playoffs Are in September

Playoffs will be held in September. If you have travel plans in September, please consider that carefully before committing to playing this season.

#4
Winner Runs Next Season

The winner of the 2026 season will be responsible for organizing and running the 2027 season. This is an important change from previous years — the commissioner can help with the tech side.

#5
Play to Win — No Splitting, No Exceptions

If you make the playoffs, the point is to play. There will be no splitting or chopping up the winnings. No exceptions.
